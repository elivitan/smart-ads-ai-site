How to Increase Average Order Value (AOV) on Shopify in 2026

Raising average order value is one of the cheapest ways to grow — the traffic is already paid for. Here are the thresholds, bundles and cross-sells that lift profit per order without eroding margin.

1. Why AOV is a growth multiplier

Average order value (AOV) is total revenue divided by orders. Raising it is one of the cheapest ways to grow: the traffic and conversion are already paid for, so every extra dollar per order flows mostly to profit — and it widens how much you can spend to acquire a customer without breaking your unit economics.

2. The tactics that raise AOV

  • Free-shipping threshold — set just above your current AOV; it's the most reliable nudge to add one more item.
  • Bundles — package complementary products at a gentle discount; raises AOV and moves slow stock.
  • Cross-sell & upsell — "frequently bought together" and a better-version upsell at the right moment.
  • Volume / tiered pricing — buy-more-save-more for consumables.
  • Post-purchase offers — a one-click add after checkout, when the buyer is already committed.

3. Protect margin while you do it

AOV for its own sake can erode profit if you discount too hard to get there. The goal is higher profit per order, not just a bigger number — bundle around margin, and keep thresholds tied to your real economics. Pricing discipline (see the pricing guide) and AOV work together.

4. Measure the right thing

Track AOV alongside gross margin per order. A tactic that lifts AOV but craters margin isn't a win. Test one change at a time and keep what raises profit per order.

Bottom line

Free-shipping thresholds, margin-aware bundles, and well-timed cross-sells raise profit per order — the cheapest growth there is, as long as you protect margin.

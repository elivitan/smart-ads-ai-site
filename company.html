Our Story

Fifteen years of agency playbooks, encoded as software.

Bamzal was built by a digital-marketing veteran with fifteen years inside the SEO and paid-media trenches. The thesis: the work a senior media buyer does is more pattern than magic — and the patterns can run themselves.

Where it started

Our founder has spent fifteen years inside performance marketing — running SEO programs, managing Google and Meta budgets, building and optimizing campaigns across every major paid channel. Most of that career was spent on the agency and consultancy side, watching the same playbook get re-applied to client after client.

Two things became clear. First, the discipline is dominated by repeatable patterns: catalog audit, competitor mapping, keyword discovery, copy testing, budget rebalancing, creative refresh, performance reporting. Second, the merchants who need this work most — small and mid-sized e-commerce stores — can't afford the retainers that fund it. Performance agencies start at $5K a month and scale up from there.

"Fifteen years of doing this taught me that 80% of a media buyer's day is pattern-matching. That part doesn't need a human. It needs a system that doesn't forget, doesn't sleep, and doesn't bill by the hour."

What we're building

Bamzal is the autonomous growth system for Shopify. A single coordinated AI runs the commercial surface of a store: paid media on five networks, organic social on six, an in-app creative studio, real-time community replies, continuous competitor intelligence, profit-aware optimization, and self-learning cycles that synthesize new rules from the outcomes of the prior week.

Every action the system takes flows through a twelve-step governance gateway, lands in an immutable decision log, and is bounded by configurable caps and seven binding production SLOs. Three autonomy tiers — shadow, observe, co-pilot — let merchants dial how much authority the AI has, independently per platform. The keys stay with the merchant. The kill switch is one click away. The system does the work.

How we built it

Three principles, baked into the architecture.

Governance before autonomy

Every platform write passes through a 12-step Mutation Gateway with rate limits, budget caps, risk scoring and a kill switch. Seven binding production SLOs pause the system if breached. CI enforces that nothing bypasses the gate.

The merchant holds the keys

Campaigns run inside your own ad accounts, not ours. OAuth scopes are minimal. The keys are revocable in one click. Every AI decision lands in an immutable log you can audit, override or roll back at any time.

Trust is earned, not granted

Three autonomy tiers per platform — shadow, observe, co-pilot. New shops start cautious. Agents auto-promote only after the system proves a 14-day Pearson correlation ≥ 0.7 between predicted and realized ROAS. Agents that drift get demoted automatically.

2024

The thesis

The repeatable parts of media buying — audit, mapping, copy, bid, rebalance, refresh, report — outnumber the genuinely judgment-heavy ones by an order of magnitude. The repeatable parts can run themselves.

Bamzal is built

Twelve specialist AI agents — each modeling a role the founder had hired (or been) over the years — wired into a single governance layer. Native Shopify embedded app, direct integrations to Google, Meta, TikTok, Pinterest and X ad APIs.

Live shops onboarded onto the autonomous-agency model. Campaign management, organic social publishing, DM and comment engagement, decision-log audit trails all running in production.

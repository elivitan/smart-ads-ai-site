1. Why the product page is the make-or-break asset

Ads, email, and social all funnel to one place: the product page. A page that converts at 1% instead of 2% silently halves the return on every channel feeding it. That's why product-page optimization is usually a better first move than buying more traffic — it lifts the whole funnel at once.

2. The product-page optimization checklist

  • Title — specific and benefit-led, not just a SKU name. Include the term a buyer would search.
  • Images — multiple high-quality shots, in-context/lifestyle, zoomable; video where it helps.
  • Description — lead with the benefit and the problem it solves, then specs. Scannable, not a wall of text.
  • Price & value — make the value obvious; show savings honestly where they exist.
  • Reviews & social proof — the single biggest trust lever on a product page.
  • Clear CTA — one prominent add-to-cart, visible without scrolling.
  • Shipping & returns — answer "when will it arrive" and "what if I don't like it" up front.
  • Objection handling — a short FAQ that kills the doubts that stop the purchase.

3. Merchandising: order matters

Which products you push forward changes your blended margin. Surfacing your best-margin, best-converting products — rather than burying them on page three of a collection — lifts profit without a single new visitor. Reorder collections so the winners lead.

4. Optimize for mobile first

Most Shopify traffic is mobile. Tap targets, fast images, a sticky add-to-cart, and a short checkout matter more than anything that only looks good on desktop.

Strong title, real images, benefit-led copy, reviews, and a frictionless mobile CTA. Fix the page and every channel feeding it performs better.
